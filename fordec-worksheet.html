FORDEC Worksheet

Use FORDEC to slow down the chaos and make a clear decision. Write short, practical notes — the goal is clarity, not perfect wording.

0 Situation Details

Set the scene. Write what is happening, where you are, and why a decision is needed.
Your responsibility at the time of the decision.
Where you are or what situation you are in.
When this occurred.
2–4 lines only. Focus on observable facts (what changed, what triggered it).

F Facts

List only what you know for sure. Keep assumptions out.
Example: what was said, what was observed, constraints, numbers, deadlines, available resources.

O Options

Write realistic choices you can actually do with your time and resources.
Include “pause / wait / do nothing” if it is a real option.

R Risks & Benefits

Compare trade-offs. What could go wrong, and what improves?
Safety, quality, time, cost, people impact, customer impact, compliance, reputation.
What gets better: clarity, stability, outcomes, safety margin, time saved, reduced errors.

D Decision

Choose one option. State it clearly and write the main reason.
One sentence: “We will ___ because ___.”

E Execution & Check

Turn the decision into action steps, then define how you will verify it’s working.
Who does what, in what order. Keep it practical and short.
What will you monitor to confirm success? What is your trigger to stop, adjust, or escalate?
